# OPP-115 Dataset Overview

## What is OPP-115?

The OPP-115 (Online Privacy Policies - 115) corpus is a dataset of website privacy policies with fine-grained human annotations. It was developed by Carnegie Mellon University under the Usable Privacy Policy Project (www.usableprivacy.org).

## Key Facts

- **Version**: 1.0 (Released 2016-07-10)
- **Number of Policies**: 115 privacy policies
- **Number of Websites**: From top US Alexa-ranked websites
- **Total Annotations**: 23,194 data practice annotations
- **Annotators**: 3 law graduate students per policy
- **Annotation Type**: Fine-grained privacy practice annotations

## Purpose

The dataset was created to enable:
- Automated analysis of privacy policies
- Understanding common privacy practices across websites
- Machine learning models for privacy policy analysis
- Research on transparency and user privacy

## Coverage

### Website Selection
- Selected from US Alexa Top 100 websites
- Covers diverse sectors (e.g., e-commerce, news, social media, finance, health)
- Policies collected between 2014-2016
- Examples: Amazon, New York Times, Instagram, PBS, Fortune

### Data Points
- 115 unique websites
- 115 privacy policies (one per website)
- Multiple annotators per policy (3 annotators)
- Annotation redundancy for quality control
